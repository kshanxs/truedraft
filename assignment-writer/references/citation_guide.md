# Citation Guide

## Purpose
Provide formatting rules for the four most common academic citation styles. Load this file only when the user requests citations or you're adding references to the output.

---

## APA 7th Edition

### In-text
- Author-date format: `(Smith, 2021)` or `Smith (2021) argues that...`
- Two authors: `(Smith & Jones, 2021)`
- Three or more: `(Smith et al., 2021)`
- Direct quote (include page): `(Smith, 2021, p. 45)`

### Reference List
```
Journal Article:
Author, A. A., & Author, B. B. (Year). Title of article. Journal Name, Volume(Issue), Page–Page. https://doi.org/xxxxx

Book:
Author, A. A. (Year). Title of work: Capital letter also for subtitle. Publisher.

Website:
Author, A. A. (Year, Month Day). Title of page. Site Name. URL
```

### Key Rules
- References listed alphabetically by surname
- Hanging indent on second and subsequent lines
- DOI as hyperlink when available
- No "Retrieved from" for stable URLs (but include for content that may change)

---

## MLA 9th Edition

### In-text
- Author-page format: `(Smith 45)` or `Smith argues that... (45)`
- No page number for websites: `(Smith)`
- No author — use shortened title: `("Article Title" 45)`

### Works Cited
```
Journal Article:
AuthorLast, First. "Title of Article." Journal Name, vol. #, no. #, Year, pp. #–#.

Book:
AuthorLast, First. Title of Book. Publisher, Year.

Website:
AuthorLast, First. "Page Title." Website Name, Day Month Year, URL.
```

### Key Rules
- "Works Cited" not "References" or "Bibliography"
- Title case for all major words in titles
- Entries listed alphabetically by author surname

---

## Harvard (Author-Date)

### In-text
- `(Smith 2021)` or `Smith (2021) states...`
- Multiple authors: `(Smith and Jones 2021)` / `(Smith et al. 2021)` for 3+
- Page for quotes: `(Smith 2021, p. 45)`

### Reference List
```
Journal Article:
Surname, Initial. (Year) 'Article title', Journal Name, Volume(Issue), pp. Page–Page.

Book:
Surname, Initial. (Year) Title of Book. Edition. City: Publisher.

Website:
Surname, Initial. (Year) 'Page title'. Available at: URL (Accessed: Day Month Year).
```

### Key Rules
- Title: books in italics, articles in single quotes
- "Available at" + "Accessed" required for web sources
- Listed alphabetically

---

## IEEE

### In-text
- Numbered in order of appearance: `[1]`, `[2, 3]`
- Refer in text as: "as shown in [3]" or "according to Smith [4]"
- Same source reused: repeat the same number

### Reference List
```
Journal Article:
[1] A. Author and B. Author, "Title of paper," Journal Abbrev., vol. #, no. #, pp. #–#, Month Year, doi: xxxxx.

Book:
[2] A. Author, Title of Book. City, State, Country: Publisher, Year, pp. #–#.

Conference Paper:
[3] A. Author, "Title," in Proc. Conf. Name, City, Year, pp. #–#.
```

### Key Rules
- Only first word of title capitalised (plus proper nouns)
- Journal names abbreviated (e.g., IEEE Trans. Neural Networks)
- Authors listed as "A. B. Surname" (initials first)
- No "et al." in reference list — list all authors

---

## When no style is specified
Use **APA 7th** as the default.

## Generating citations from claims
When adding citations to an essay that has factual claims but no references:
1. Identify sentences making specific factual, theoretical, or statistical claims
2. Add a placeholder citation `[CITE: description of claim]` inline
3. At the end, generate a "References" section with plausible structured citations in the requested format
4. Note to user: "Citations marked [CITE] are placeholder references — verify against your actual sources before submitting."
