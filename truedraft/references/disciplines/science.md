# Discipline: Science & Engineering

## Load when
The user's course is: Biology, Chemistry, Physics, Computer Science, Engineering, Medicine, Environmental Science, Mathematics, or any natural/applied science field.

---

## Structure — IMRAD

Science and engineering assignments almost always follow IMRAD or a derivative:

```
Introduction      ← Why this problem matters; what gap exists
Methods           ← How the study/experiment/algorithm was conducted
Results           ← What was found (data, outputs, observations)
Discussion        ← What it means; how it compares to existing work
Conclusion        ← Summary + implications + future work
```

For non-experimental reports (reviews, literature surveys), adapt to:
```
Introduction → Background → Analysis → Discussion → Conclusion
```

---

## Writing Rules

### Voice
- **Passive voice is standard** for methods: "The samples were heated to..." not "We heated the samples to..."
- Active voice is acceptable in discussion and conclusion: "This finding suggests..."
- Avoid first-person "I" entirely unless the assignment explicitly asks for reflection

### Hedging
Science writing uses hedging language to signal uncertainty appropriately:
- "This suggests..." / "The data indicate..." / "It appears that..."
- Avoid absolute claims unless supported by overwhelming evidence
- "This proves X" → "This is consistent with X"

### Quantification
- Use specific numbers where available: "a 23% reduction" not "a significant reduction"
- Include units always: 45°C, 300ms, 12kB
- Introduce abbreviations on first use: "machine learning (ML)"

### Evidence before interpretation
In science writing, always state the evidence before interpreting it:
> ❌ "The algorithm is highly efficient. The runtime was O(n log n)."
> ✅ "The algorithm achieved O(n log n) runtime, demonstrating efficiency suitable for large-scale deployment."

---

## Introduction Rules

Open with the research gap or problem context — not a definition:
> ✅ "Despite advances in image segmentation techniques, real-time performance on edge devices remains a significant constraint..."
> ❌ "Image segmentation is defined as the process of partitioning a digital image..."

---

## Citation Defaults
- **Preferred:** IEEE (engineering), APA 7 (sciences), Vancouver (medicine)
- Always cite methods and data sources; never cite common knowledge

---

## Originality Notes
In science writing, methods sections are the highest-risk — they often sound identical across submissions because the same procedure is being described.

Counter strategies:
- Focus on *why* each step was taken, not just what was done
- Vary the order in which procedural details are presented
- Note any deviations from standard protocol
