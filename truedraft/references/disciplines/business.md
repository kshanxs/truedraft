# Discipline: Business

## Load when
The user's course is: Business Administration, Management, Marketing, Finance, Accounting, Economics, Entrepreneurship, HRM, Operations, Strategy, or any business/management field.

---

## Structure — Executive Summary First

Business writing leads with the conclusion, not the argument. Decision-makers read top-down.

```
Executive Summary   ← Key finding + recommendation in 1–2 paragraphs
Introduction        ← Context and scope of the analysis
Analysis            ← Evidence, data, frameworks applied
Discussion          ← Implications, trade-offs, risks
Recommendation      ← Specific, actionable, justified
Conclusion          ← Summary + next steps
References
Appendices          ← (if needed) raw data, full calculations
```

For essays (not reports):
```
Introduction → Theoretical Framework → Analysis → Critical Evaluation → Conclusion
```

## Writing Rules

### Quantify Everything Possible
Business writing is most credible with numbers. Vague claims are weak.

> ❌ "The company performed well in recent years."
> ✅ "Revenue grew 34% CAGR between 2019–2023, driven primarily by APAC market expansion."

### Framework-Driven Analysis
Business assignments expect you to apply known frameworks correctly:
- **SWOT** — Strengths, Weaknesses, Opportunities, Threats
- **Porter's Five Forces** — competitive landscape
- **PESTLE** — macro-environmental factors
- **BCG Matrix** — portfolio analysis
- **Ansoff Matrix** — growth strategy

Don't just list frameworks — apply them to the specific case/company being analysed.

### Recommendations Must Be Actionable
Vague recommendations fail in business writing:
> ❌ "The company should improve its marketing strategy."
> ✅ "The company should allocate 15–20% of its Q3 marketing budget to short-form video content targeting the 18–34 demographic on TikTok and Instagram Reels, where competitor X currently dominates awareness metrics."

### Concise, Direct Sentences
Business writing values clarity over sophistication. Short sentences, direct verbs.
> ❌ "It is recommended that consideration be given to the possibility of..."
> ✅ "We recommend..."

---

## Introduction Rules

State the scope and purpose clearly — not a broad thesis like humanities:
> ✅ "This report analyses the strategic position of [Company] in the [Industry] market using Porter's Five Forces and SWOT frameworks, with a focus on [specific problem]. Three strategic options are evaluated, with a recommended course of action."
> ❌ "Business strategy is an important area of study that companies must consider..."

---

## Citation Defaults
- **Preferred:** Harvard (most business schools), APA 7
- Cite all data sources: market reports, company filings, academic studies
- Annual reports and industry data are primary sources in business — use them

---

## Originality Notes
Case study analyses are the highest risk in business — students often describe companies in the same terms using the same sources.

Counter strategies:
- Lead with your own analytical frame before presenting facts
- Link data to frameworks explicitly: "This revenue concentration (80% from one customer) represents high dependency risk under Porter's buyer power lens..."
- Use your own synthesis of multiple sources rather than paraphrasing one report
