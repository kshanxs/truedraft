# Discipline: Commerce & Business Studies

## Load when
The user's course is: Commerce, Business Studies, Economics (A-level or introductory undergraduate), Accounting, Marketing (introductory), Entrepreneurship (school-level), or any school/foundation-level business or economics course.

> **Note:** If the assignment is a corporate case study, MBA-level strategy report, or advanced management analysis, load `references/disciplines/business.md` instead. This file is calibrated for school-level and introductory undergraduate commerce work.

## Structure

### For Commerce / Business Studies Essays
```
Introduction          ← Define scope; state the argument or position
Theory Application    ← Identify the concept; apply it to the scenario
Evidence              ← Data, examples, real-world cases
Evaluation            ← Judgements, trade-offs, "however" and "on the other hand"
Conclusion            ← Summarise and state a supported judgement
```

### For Economics Essays
```
Introduction → Define Key Terms → Apply Theory → Diagrams (if relevant) → Evaluation → Conclusion
```

### For Accounting / Finance Tasks
```
Statement of Purpose → Calculations / Financial Statements → Analysis → Recommendations
```
> Show all workings. State the formula before applying it.

### For Business Reports (school-level)
```
Executive Overview → Situation Analysis → Options → Recommendation → Conclusion
```

## Writing Rules

### Theory-First Writing
Identify the economics or business concept, then apply it to the specific scenario:
> ✅ "Price elasticity of demand (PED) measures the responsiveness of quantity demanded to a change in price. In this case, petrol has a PED of approximately −0.2, indicating inelastic demand — consumers have few substitutes in the short run, so a 10% price rise leads to only a 2% fall in quantity demanded."
> ❌ "When petrol prices rise, people still buy it because they need it."

### Evaluation Is Non-Negotiable
Commerce examiners award marks specifically for **evaluation** — balanced judgements that acknowledge limitations, trade-offs, and conditions under which conclusions may differ.

Use these constructions deliberately:
- "However, this assumes..."
- "In the short run... but in the long run..."
- "This is more likely to apply to... than to..."
- "The extent to which this holds depends on..."
- "On the other hand..."

> ❌ "Therefore, the minimum wage increase will reduce unemployment."
> ✅ "In the short run, the minimum wage increase may reduce unemployment by raising consumer spending power. However, if labour demand is elastic, firms may substitute labour for capital, reducing employment in the long run. The net effect depends on the elasticity of labour demand in the specific industry."

### Diagrams (Economics)
When a diagram is relevant:
- Reference it explicitly: "As shown in Figure 1, the supply curve shifts rightward..."
- Explain what changed, why, and what the new equilibrium implies
- Label axes, curves, and equilibrium points — describe these in text if you cannot draw them

### Quantification in Commerce
Support claims with numbers where available:
- Use percentages, ratios, and index figures
- For accounting: always show formula → values → result → interpretation
> ✅ "The current ratio = Current Assets / Current Liabilities = £240,000 / £120,000 = 2.0. This indicates the firm can cover its short-term liabilities twice over, suggesting adequate liquidity."
> ❌ "The current ratio looks fine."

### Frameworks and Concepts to Apply
**Economics:**
- Demand / Supply analysis (shifts vs. movements along the curve)
- Price, Income, and Cross Elasticities of Demand (PED, YED, XED)
- Market structures: perfect competition, monopolistic competition, oligopoly, monopoly
- Externalities, public goods, market failure
- Fiscal / monetary policy tools

**Business Studies:**
- Ansoff Matrix (growth strategies)
- SWOT analysis (applied to a specific business, not generic)
- Stakeholder analysis
- Break-even analysis (TR = TC; margin of safety)
- Motivation theories: Maslow, Herzberg, Taylor

**Accounting:**
- Ratio analysis: liquidity (current ratio, acid test), profitability (gross/net profit margin, ROCE), gearing
- Income statements, balance sheets, cash flow statements
- Depreciation (straight-line vs. reducing balance)

Apply frameworks to the *specific scenario* — never just list them abstractly.

### Concise, Clear Sentences
Avoid overly academic or vague language. Commerce writing should be logical and direct:
> ❌ "There is a possibility that the aforementioned policy intervention could have certain negative implications for various stakeholders."
> ✅ "The subsidy may benefit producers but raise costs for taxpayers."

## Introduction Rules

Define the key term or concept, then state what the essay will argue or analyse:
> ✅ "A monopoly exists when a single firm dominates a market with significant barriers to entry. This essay argues that while monopolies can enable economies of scale, the welfare losses from reduced consumer surplus and higher prices typically outweigh these benefits in consumer goods markets."
> ❌ "In this essay I will discuss monopolies and their effects."

For accounting tasks, state the purpose and scope:
> ✅ "This report analyses the financial performance of [Company] for the year ended 31 March 2024, using profitability, liquidity, and gearing ratios. Recommendations are made regarding its suitability for a short-term loan."

## Citation Defaults
- **Preferred:** Harvard (most commerce and economics courses), APA 7
- Cite textbooks, government data (ONS, World Bank, IMF), and news sources for real-world examples
- For economics essays, cite any statistics or data used to support claims

## Originality Notes
The highest-risk areas in commerce writing are:
1. **Definition dumps** — copying textbook definitions verbatim
2. **Generic framework application** — "SWOT shows strengths, weaknesses..." without linking to the specific business
3. **Conclusion without evaluation** — restating the introduction rather than making a supported judgement

Counter strategies:
- Paraphrase definitions in your own words, then immediately apply the concept
- Tie every framework point to a specific detail from the case/scenario
- Build evaluation into the body paragraphs, not just the conclusion — every claim should be qualified or contested
- Use real-world examples or data to anchor generic theory: "This mirrors the UK government's 2022 energy price cap, which..."
